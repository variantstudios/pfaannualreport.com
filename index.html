Mission

To protect life and property by being prompt, skillful, and caring. Our actions are anchored in the core values of courage, leadership and duty.

The Poudre Fire Authority is committed to saving lives and property as well as being transparent with our operations and budget. The following data and overview is based on performance measurements, programed activities, operational reports, revenue and expenditures that occurred in 2014. Accreditation

Accreditation

Quality improvement and service evaluation in order to achieve Commission on Fire Accreditation International (CFAI) accreditation. Application for accreditation is complete, site visit is scheduled for second quarter 2015.

Fire Code Adoption

Fire Code Adoption

Increase citizen and firefighter safety through the built environment. The 2012 International Fire Code was adopted and ratified by community leaders.

Performance Evaluations

Performance Evaluations

Align process with Forging a Courageous Organization program. The new performance evaluation system affects all PFA employees; the first round of performance reviews took place in late 2014.

COMPREHENSIVE ANNUAL FINANCIAL REPORT

Comprehensive Annual Financial Report

Transparent and accountable to the community. Qualified for Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) Certificate of Achievement for Excellence in Financial Reporting.

IMPROVE SAFETY PROGRAMS

Improve Safety Programs

Enhance current health and safety program for employees. A new electronic reporting form and personal review system has been activated that track injuries and helps identify mitigation efforts, thereby decreasing PFA member injuries.

PUBLIC EDUCATOR

Public Educator

Enhance public education efforts with a concentration on high risk populations. A public educator has been hired and is developing innovative outreach strategies that will increase community preparedness and participation.

EMS SERVICES AGREEMENT

EMS Services Agreement

Ensure continued, high-quality ambulance services for the region, including PFA boundaries, through a competitive process and contractual agreement.

INTERGOVERNMENTAL AGREEMENT/REVENUE ALLOCATION FORMULA

Intergovernmental Agreement/Revenue Allocation Formula

Provide transparent finance reports to assure stable and predictable funding streams. Poudre Valley Fire Protection and the City have approved a newly updated IGA.

WILDLAND/VOLUNTEER COORDINATOR

Wildland/Volunteer Coordinator

Improve the Authority’s ability to respond and recover from natural and man-made disasters by hiring a wildland coordinator who also performs volunteer coordinator duties. This position has been filled.

Stories from 2014

OVERHEAD SPRINKLER

HOME SAVED BY SPRINKLER HEAD

February 11 – 1731 Morningside Drive – A fire broke out in an upstairs utility room, where combustible materials were stored near a water heater. The multi-family unit is protected by an automatic fire sprinkler system. The fire was controlled by a single sprinkler head which limited fire damage to $1,000. Fire sprinklers do make a difference!

GRASS FIRE

6720 NORTH COUNTY ROAD 21

Station 7 units were dispatched to a report of a grass fire; while enroute they noted flames above the tree tops and requested Station 12 units to assist. As Engine 7 proceeded up the driveway it was determined that a structure was involved. The fire was caused by an extension cord used as permanent wiring. The structure was a total loss with damage estimated at $300,000.

FALLEN HIKER

HORSETOOTH MOUNTAIN ROCK

Just after sunrise, crews received a call for help at Horsetooth Mountain Rock where a hiker was reported to have fallen in one of the crevices. Enroute to the call it was learned that the hiker had fallen more than sixty feet off the rock and was injured. This rescue was a collaboration of PFA, Larimer County Parks, Larimer County Search and Rescue, and University Colorado Health.
